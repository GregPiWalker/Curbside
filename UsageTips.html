Configuring Curbside

Curbside offers several settings for customized behavior. To access them, close Curbside if it is open and then open the Settings app. Scroll down until you find Curbside listed in the Apps section.  Tap on the "Curbside" row and you will be presented with all of the available settings.

Setting the Visual Theme

You may choose between several different visual themes.  Open the Curbside settings, then tap on the "Select Theme" row and then choose which theme you want to use.

top
Setting the Default Email Address

If you choose to set a default email address in the Curbside settings, then the "TO:" field of exported visit log emails will be filled in automatically. To do so, go to the Curbside settings and type your email address into the field labeled "Send Report To".

Setting Automatic Reporting Behavior

You may configure Curbside to compose a visit report automatically as soon as a new visit is saved. To do so, go to the Curbside settings and toggle the switch labeled "New Visit" under the "Send Automatic Report For" section.

Logging a New Visit

To log a new visit, press the "New Visit" button from the app's main view. A new visit requires a patient name and a chief complaint. The current date and time are automatically selected as the new visit's creation timestamp, but you may back-date the visit by tapping on the date-time field and selecting the desired value. Since all values on a visit log except for the patient can be modified, you may fill in the remaining fields immediately or later.

As you type in the patient name, comparisons are made against your existing patient database. Partial matches are displayed in a list below the name field. If the patient for whom you are logging a visit appears in the list, simply select that row and the name will be filled in for you. If a recognized patient name appears in the name field, a button which allows you to view the patient record will become visible to the right of the text box.

The new visit log is not saved until you press the "Done" button at the top right of the navigation bar. Pressing the back navigation button will discard the log that is under construction.

Editing a Logged Visit

You may make changes to a visit after it has been logged. Load an existing visit into the "Visit" view and press the "Edit" button at the top right in the navigation bar. While the patient's name cannot be changed directly, all other values including the timestamp can be modified as you wish.

The changes will not be saved until the "Save" button is pressed in the top right of the navigation bar. Pressing the back navigation button will cause all changes to be lost.

Adding a Prescription to a Visit

Any number of prescriptions optionally may be added to a new or existing visit. To do so, tap the "New Visit" button or load an existing visit into the "Visit" view and press the "Edit" button at the top right. Scroll down to the bottom of the "New Visit" or "Edit Visit" view and press the green "+" button just left of the table row that says "Add Prescription".

The new prescriptions will not be saved until the "Save" button is pressed in the "New Visit" or "Edit Visit" view.

top
Removing a Prescription from a Visit

Prescriptions may be removed from a new or existing visit. From either the "New Visit" view or the "Edit Visit" view, Scroll down to the bottom and press the red "-" button just left of row holding the prescription you wish to remove. Once the "Delete" button appears in the prescription's row, press it.

Removal of prescriptions will not be saved until the "Save" button is pressed in the "New Visit" or "Edit Visit" view.

Deleting a Logged Visit

Presently, visits can only be deleted one-by-one. Tap the "Visit Log" button on the main page. Here, you can delete a visit by swiping from left to right on the offending row. The swipe will cause a "Delete" button to appear in the row. Tapping the button will delete the visit immediately and irretrievably. If you decide not to delete the visit before pressing the button, tapping anywhere else on the screen will cause the "Delete" button to vanish.

Adding a Patient Record

There are several ways to add a new patient. For convenience, a new patient record is automatically created at the same time that a new visit is logged, if the patient name given to the "New Visit" view does not already exist in the patient database. This allows you to log a visit rapidly without worrying about patient details until later. In this case, the new patient is not actually saved until the new visit is also saved. An interruption in creation of the new visit will prevent a new patient record from being saved. Alternatively, new patients can be created by pressing the "+" button in the upper right-hand corner of the "My Patients" view.

While creating a new patient record, you may choose to add additional information. Any number of allergies, current known medications, and pharmacies may be added. Neither allergies nor medications are related to prescriptions yet, but at some point I hope to associate them. This will give the benefit of warnings when a prescription is given for a known allergen, as well as a searchable list of medications.

The new patient will not be saved until the "Done" button is pressed. Pressing "Cancel" instead will discard the new patient record.

Editing a Patient Record

You may edit an existing patient record from two different starting points. Beginning from the "My Patients" view, you can select a patient and then press the "Edit" button on the right side of the navigation bar. This will open the "Edit Patient" view. Alternatively, if you are viewing an existing visit in the "Visit" view, you may then view the patient record by pressing the blue ">" button to the right of the patient's name. This presents the "Patient" view, from which you may press the "Edit" button on the right side of the navigation bar.

As you edit a patient, you may add or remove allergies, medications

Edited values will not be permanent until you press the "Done" button in the "Edit Patient" view. If you press the back button labeled "Patient", your changes will be lost.

Importing Patient Information from the Contacts app

You can import patient details from existing people in your Apple Contacts database. This feature is enabled by a hidden toolbar in both the "New Patient" view and the "Edit Patient" view. Expose the toolbar by first ensuring that the scrollable area is scrolled all the way to the top, removing all fingers from the display, and then scrolling the view down again. This will make the toolbar slide down below the navigation bar. If the toolbar appears by accident, simply press the "Cancel" button on its left side. To continue with the data import operation, press the "->" button on its right side. This will open the Apple Contact list. You may cancel at this point by pressing the "Cancel" button on the left of the navigation bar. Otherwise, tap on the row holding the desired contact. The Apple Contacts app will disappear.

Importing patient data has the effect of erasing information that already exists in the patient record if the same information was found in the Apple Contact record. However, these changes are not saved until you press the "Done" button on the right side ot the navigation bar in either the "Edit Patient" or "New Patient" view.

Deleting a Patient Record

At this time, a patient record can only be deleted from the "Edit Patient" view. With a future release, it will be possible to swipe a row in the "My Patients" view in order to summon a delete button for that row. Until then, load the patient to be deleted into the "Edit Patient" view, scroll to the bottom and press the "Delete Patient" button. Once you press the "Confirm" button in the "Confirm Delete" dialog, the patient record will be deleted along with all related data, including visit logs.

Adding a Pharmacy to a Patient

A patient record may have any number of pharmacies associated to it. This can be useful, if only to store the phone number for your convenience. Pharmacy entries can be added to an existing patient at any time in the "Edit Patient" view or while creating a new patient in the "New Patient" view. In either view, scroll down until you see the Pharmacy section. Tap on the green "+" button to the left of the row that says "Add Pharmacy". This summons the "New Pharmacy" view. A pharmacy record requires a name and a phone number.

The new pharmacy will not be saved until you press the "Done" button in the navigation toolbar. If you press the back navigational button, the new pharmacy will be discarded.

top
Deleting a Pharmacy from a Patient

At this time, there is one way to delete a pharmacy from a patient record. Select the patient from the "My Patients" view. Pressing the "Edit" button in the navigation toolbar will bring you to the "Edit Patient" view. Scroll down until the pharmacy section is visible. Presson the red"-" button to the left of the pharmacy that you want to remove. A red "Delete" button will appear inside the pharmacy's row. Press it and the pharmacy will be removed.

The above changes will not become permanent until you press the "Done" button at the top right of the navigation toolbar. Pressing the back navigational button will cause any pharmacy deletions to be forgotten.

Setting a Patient's Default Pharmacy

A patient's default pharmacy is denoted by a checkmark in the "Patient" and "Edit Patient" views. If a patient record has more than one pharmacy associated with it, you have the option of setting which one is the default pharmacy in the "Patient" view. Press and hold your finger on the row of the desired default pharmacy. When the context menu appears, tap on the words "Set As Default". The phone number for the default pharmacy is the one used to call in a prescription.

Calling in Prescriptions

Once a patient record has one or more pharmacies associated to it, any visit log under that patient's name can be used to easily call the patient's default pharmacy without dialing a phone number.

There are two ways to dial a pharmacy. First, open an existing visit in the "Visit" view by selecting it from the "Visit Log" view. Alternatively, you can view the visit by first viewing the patient, scrolling down to the "Visits" section and then tapping on the desired visit. Once you are in the "Visit" view, you should see a toolbar at the bottom. Pressing the "Call It In" button on the right side will summon the phone dialer and automatically dial the phone number saved in the patient's default pharmacy.

You can also dial a pharmacy while viewing an existing patient in the "Patient" view, so long as that patient has at least one pharmacy. Scroll the view down to the bottom until you see the "Pharmacies" section. Perform a long-press on the pharmacy you wish to call. A menu will pop-up with an option to "Call Now". Tap on that option to initiate the call.

Generating Visit Reports

A visit report contains all information pertinent to a specific visit, including prescriptions. Patient information is kept to a minimum. Visits reports can be compiled on demand and delivered via email. This feature requires that you have previously configured an email account on your device. Currently, you can export an individual visit or the complete visit log for an individual patient. If you have provided your email address in the Curbside settings, then the destination address for the report email will be filled in automatically.

To send a report on a single visit, open an existing visit in the "Visit" view by selecting it from the "Visit Log" view. Alternatively, you can open the visit by first viewing the patient, scrolling down to the "Visits" section and then tapping on the desired visit. Press the "Report" button on the left side of the bottom toolbar.

To send a report on all existing visits for a single patient, open the "My Patients" view and select the desired patient. If the patient has one or more visit logs, there will be a toolbar present at the bottom of the view. Press the "Report Visits" button on the left side of the bottom toolbar.

Pressing either of the report buttons will open the Apple Email app and compose a new email message with a subject, message body and, depending on the Curbside settings, a destination address. The report is also attached to the email as an HTML file for convenience. You need only press send in order for the report to be delivered.

Backing Up Data

As of version 1.2, you can backup all of your Curbside data to an archive file.  You have a choice of saving the archive in the app's "Documents" folder, or sending the archive to yourself as an attachment in an email message.  Archives that are saved to the filesystem can be accessed via iTunes file sharing.

To create a backup, press the "More" button and then press the "Backup Data" button.  You will be presented with a dialog asking whether you want to send the back up in an email message.  If you choose "No", the archived data will be saved to the app's shared "Documents" folder.  If you press yes, the Apple Mail app will open with a new email message.  Press "Send" to send the email with the data attached.

top
Importing Saved Data

As of version 1.2, you can import Curbside data that was saved as in a backup archive.  This is an effective means for ensuring that all of your Apple devices are using the same Curbside data.  When you import saved data, only the data that does not yet exist will be imported.  This way new patients and visit records are imported without altering pre-existing entries.  You can keep two (or more) devices in sync by backing up Curbside data from both devices and then importing those data into the respective opposite device.  Importing saved data is also helpful if, for some reason, your Curbside app was deleted or lost.  In that case, you can simply download Curbside again and import the previously backed-up data to restore your app to normal.

To import saved data, you must use the Apple Mail app on the device that will read the data.  If you have emailed the data backup to yourself, simple view that email message.  Otherwise, copy the Curbside backup file from the app's shared folder in iTunes, attach it to an email message and send the message to yourself.  Once the email message is open, tap on the attached file.  You will be presented with a choice to open the file using Curbside.  If you select that option, Mail will close and Curbside will open with all of the new data merged into your existing data.

Sending Feedback

Your feedback regarding Curbside is much appreciated. We are open to feature suggestions, ideas for improvement and friendly greetings. Please be patient and remember that no software is perfect.

First, press the "More" button and then press the "About" button. At the bottom of the "About" view, there is a button labeled "Send Feedback". This feature requires that you have previously configured an email account on your device. Pressing the button will open an email addressed to the developer and prepopulated with some information about your device. Add your comments to the message and press send.
